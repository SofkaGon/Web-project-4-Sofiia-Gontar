Naawi-Oodena

Naawi Oodena means "New Community" or "New Land" in Anishinaabemowin, reflecting a fresh beginning rooted in Indigenous culture and values. It is a community being built on the former Kapyong Barracks site, designed to bring together Indigenous people and others in a place where culture, economy, and environment are all respected. Naawi Oodena focuses on creating safe, healthy, and connected neighborhoods that honor Indigenous traditions and support healing and growth. Community Space

These are parks, green areas, trails, and gardens throughout the neighborhood. They offer places to relax, meet friends, and enjoy nature. These spaces also help make the area more welcoming and beautiful. For Indigenous communities, community spaces provide places to connect with the land, practice cultural activities, and build a sense of belonging.

Culture

Naawi Oodena is designed to be a place where Indigenous culture is visible, celebrated, and shared. It supports cultural healing by creating spaces for ceremonies, storytelling, and gatherings that strengthen community bonds. By including Indigenous art, language, and traditions in the design and daily life of the community, Naawi Oodena helps keep these important parts of identity alive for future generations. This cultural focus not only benefits Indigenous residents but also builds understanding and respect among all people living in and visiting the community.

One important feature is the ceremonial grounds, where traditional practices like smudging and seasonal celebrations can take place.

The site also includes public art created by Indigenous artists that reflects the history and stories of the Treaty One Nations.
